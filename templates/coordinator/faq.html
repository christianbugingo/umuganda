Frequently Asked Questions

Umuganda is a Rwandan tradition of community service where people come together to work on community projects. It typically occurs on the last Saturday of each month and helps maintain community infrastructure, clean public spaces, and foster unity among citizens.

First, create an account by clicking "Register" in the navigation. Then browse available projects on the "Projects" page. Click on any project to view details, and if you're interested, click the "Sign Up" button. You'll receive confirmation and the project will appear in your dashboard.

Most projects require basic work tools like gloves, hats for sun protection, sturdy shoes, and water bottles. Some projects may have specific requirements listed in the project description. Always check the project details before attending.

Please notify the project coordinator as soon as possible if you cannot attend. You can contact them through the platform or directly. This allows them to find replacement volunteers and ensures the project can proceed successfully.

Projects are organized by Rwanda's administrative divisions: Province, District, Sector, Cell, and Village (Umudugudu). This ensures projects are accessible to local community members and address specific local needs.

Currently, projects are created by community coordinators and administrators. If you have a project idea, please contact your local community leader or use the contact form to suggest it. We may add community project proposal features in the future.

No, participation in Umuganda projects is completely free. The platform is designed to make community service accessible to all Rwandan citizens. Some projects may provide refreshments or small incentives, but these are not guaranteed.

Your volunteer hours are tracked in your dashboard. After attending a project, coordinators mark your attendance and log the hours contributed. You can view your total hours and project history anytime in your dashboard.

The platform is designed for all Rwandan citizens interested in community service. You must be 18 or older to create an account, or have parental consent if younger. Community coordinators and administrators have additional access to manage projects.

You can contact us through the information in the footer: email info@umuganda.rw or call +250 788 123 456. For project-specific questions, contact the project coordinator directly through the project details page.
